Comparative Study of Lightweight CNN Models on Fashion-MNIST

September 2025 – December 2025 (Individual Project)

Problem: Selecting an optimal lightweight CNN architecture for mobile or embedded vision tasks requires balancing accuracy and computational efficiency. Most benchmarks are based on large datasets like ImageNet, which may not reflect performance on smaller, domain-specific datasets.

Data: Fashion-MNIST dataset (70,000 grayscale images of 28×28 pixels across 10 clothing categories).

Approach: Compared three mainstream lightweight architectures—MobileNetV2, ShuffleNetV2, and EfficientNet-B0—under strictly controlled training conditions (30 epochs, SGD optimizer, cosine annealing). Evaluated models using accuracy, parameter count, FLOPs, and inference time.

Outcome/Impact: All three models achieved ~93.1% accuracy. MobileNetV2 offered the best efficiency (0.81M params, 8.09M FLOPs, 8.96 ms inference), while EfficientNet-B0 was over-parameterized for this task. The study provides actionable guidance for lightweight model selection on small-scale vision tasks.

Your contribution: Sole developer — designed experiment, implemented training pipeline in PyTorch, conducted statistical analysis across three random seeds, and authored full report.
